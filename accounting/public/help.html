1. Signing In

Go to strikepointsims.com and click "Continue with Google." Use your business Gmail account. You'll stay signed in on that device — click Sign Out in the sidebar when you're done on a shared computer.

Both Mike and Jill can log in. To share the same set of books, use the same Gmail account. To keep separate records, use different Gmail accounts.

2. The Dashboard

Your financial home screen. It shows at a glance:

  • YTD Income — all money received this calendar year
  • YTD Expenses — all money spent this year
  • Net P/L — profit or loss so far (income minus expenses)
  • Pending Reimbursements — what the business owes you for personal card charges
  • Monthly Cash Flow chart — income vs. expenses by month
  • Expenses by Category — where the money is going
  • Tax Alerts — upcoming quarterly estimated tax due dates and 1099 warnings
  • Budget Health — how spending tracks against your targets (if budgets are set)
  • Quick-Log widget — add a simple expense in seconds without opening a full panel

The Dashboard updates live — every transaction you add immediately changes the numbers.

3. Logging Transactions

Tap + in the top bar (mobile) or + Add Transaction (desktop). Fill in the core fields:

  • Expense or Income — toggle at the top of the panel
  • Amount — always a positive number
  • Description — brief note (e.g. "Comcast monthly bill", "Founding member John Smith")
  • Category — pick the closest match. The app will suggest one based on the vendor name as you type
  • Date — defaults to today; change it if entering something from earlier
  • Payment Method — which account or card you used:
    • Business Account — LLC checking or business credit card
    • Personal Card — your personal credit or debit card (automatically flagged for reimbursement)
    • SBA Funds — money from your SBA loan draw
    • Owner Capital — money you personally invested into the business

More Options

Tap "▾ More options" to expand advanced fields:

  • Vendor — link to a vendor you've created (enables 1099 tracking)
  • Customer — link income transactions to specific members
  • CT Sales Tax — check if you collected CT sales tax on income
  • Loan Payment Split — appears when category is "Interest — Loan". Split the total into interest (tax-deductible) and principal (not deductible). This is important for accurate tax reporting
  • Link to Capital Asset — for equipment purchases, connect to the asset in your register
  • Notes — any additional details

Tip: The app remembers the category you chose for each vendor. Next time you type the same vendor name, it'll suggest the category automatically.

4. Attaching Receipts

Every expense over $75 should have a receipt attached. The IRS requires it, and your CPA will ask for it. You can attach a receipt in three ways:

  • Drag and drop — drag a file from your computer onto the receipt zone in the panel
  • Browse — click the receipt zone to open a file picker (JPG, PNG, PDF supported)
  • Take Photo (mobile) — on iPhone or Android, tap "📷 Take Photo" to use your camera directly

Receipts are stored securely in the cloud — they're not stuck on one device. You can view them from any browser by clicking the 📎 icon next to a transaction.

If you don't have the receipt yet, save the transaction without it and come back later. The Missing Receipts report shows you every expense over $75 that still needs documentation.

5. Personal Card Reimbursements

When you use your personal card for a business expense, log it exactly like any other transaction — but select "Personal Card" as the payment method. The app automatically flags it as "Pending Reimbursement" so you don't lose track of what the business owes you.

When you transfer money from the business account to yourself to cover it:

  1. Go to Reports → Reimbursements
  2. Find the transaction and click "Mark Reimbursed"

This closes the loop and keeps both sets of books clean. The Dashboard always shows your current pending reimbursement total so you know exactly what you're owed.

6. Customers

Track your members, walk-ins, and corporate clients. Go to Customers → + Add Customer. Fields include name, email, phone, type (Member / Walk-in / Corporate), membership tier, start date, and status.

Once a customer exists, you can link income transactions to them (in the transaction panel under "More options"). Click any customer row to see their full transaction history and lifetime value.

This is useful for membership retention analysis and for understanding which revenue streams are strongest.

7. Vendors & 1099 Tracking

Track everyone you pay — your landlord, attorney, accountant, TrackMan, Comcast, etc. Go to Vendors → + Add Vendor.

1099 Tracking (Important)

If you pay an individual or unincorporated business more than $600 per year for services, you're legally required to file a 1099-NEC by January 31. Here's how to track it correctly:

  1. Create the vendor in the Vendors section
  2. In the vendor panel, tap "▾ More details" to expand the 1099 section
  3. Enter their EIN or SSN
  4. Enable "1099 Eligible"
  5. Link your expense transactions to that vendor

The app tracks their running total and alerts you on the Dashboard when they cross $600. The 1099 Vendors report shows all eligible vendors, their YTD payments, and which ones are missing an EIN — everything your accountant needs to file the forms.

Note: Payments to corporations (Inc., LLC taxed as corp) don't require 1099s. Payments to individuals and partnerships do. When in doubt, ask for a W-9.

8. Time Tracking & Mileage

Logging Hours

Go to Time → + Log Time. Fill in:

  • Date — defaults to today
  • Hours — use decimals (1.5 = 1 hour 30 minutes)
  • Description — what you were doing ("Reviewed contractor bids", "Called SBA bank")
  • Category — the area of the business (Operations, Marketing, Finance, etc.)

Why It Matters

Time logs document that you're an active participant in the business — relevant for LLC and SBA compliance, and for supporting home office deductions. Your CPA will want the "Hours by Category" CSV at year end.

Logging Mileage

Go to Time → Mileage tab → + Log Mileage. Enter miles, purpose, and optional from/to locations. The app calculates your IRS deduction automatically at the current rate ($0.70/mile for 2026). Check the "Round trip" checkbox to double the miles automatically.

The Mileage Log report exports a CPA-ready CSV with total miles, per-trip details, and total deduction.

9. Recurring Transactions

For expenses that repeat every month (rent, SBA payment, subscriptions), set up a recurring template so you don't have to remember to log them manually.

Setting Up

  1. Go to Settings → Recurring → + Add Recurring
  2. Fill in the amount, category, payment method, and how often it repeats
  3. Set the day of month it's due (e.g. day 1 = first of every month)
  4. Toggle Active when the recurring charge actually starts

Two templates come pre-loaded: the SBA loan payment and monthly rent. They're set to inactive — toggle them active when payments begin.

Confirming Drafts

When a recurring transaction is due, a banner appears on the Dashboard: "3 recurring transactions are ready to confirm." Tap it, review the list, and click Confirm. The transaction is saved and the next due date advances automatically. Click Skip to skip that month without saving.

10. Bank CSV Import

Rather than typing every transaction by hand, download a CSV export from your bank's website and import it.

  1. Go to Transactions → Import CSV
  2. Upload the CSV file from your bank (Liberty Bank, M&T, etc.)
  3. Match the columns: Date, Description, Amount (the app shows a preview)
  4. Click Import — transactions appear in a "Needs Review" queue
  5. In the queue, assign categories and confirm each transaction

The app remembers your column mapping, so the second import from the same bank requires no re-mapping. It also suggests categories based on vendor names as you review.

Import once a month to catch anything you missed logging manually — your bank statement is the source of truth.

11. Budget Tracking

Set monthly spending targets per category in Settings → Budgets. The Dashboard Budget Health card turns green when you're on track, amber when you've used 75%+, and red when you're over budget.

The Budget vs. Actual report shows a detailed comparison for all categories with budgets set.

12. Reports

Go to Reports to see the full library of 20 report cards. Click any card to open it. Every report has Export CSV and/or Print options.

Key Reports

  • Profit & Loss — your income statement. The primary report for your CPA. Organized by IRS Schedule C categories
  • Tax Deductibility — shows which expenses are 100% deductible, which are 50% (meals), and which are non-deductible (owner draws)
  • 1099 Vendors — who needs a 1099 form, their EIN status, and YTD payment totals
  • Missing Receipts — expenses over $75 with no receipt attached. Clean this up before sending anything to your CPA
  • Reimbursements — personal card charges and their status (pending vs. reimbursed)
  • Loan Amortization — payment schedule for SBA and TrackMan loans, showing interest paid (deductible) vs. principal
  • Runway & Burn Rate — how long your current cash position lasts at your current spending rate
  • CT Sales Tax — taxable revenue and 6.35% tax collected, for CT DRS filing
  • Period Comparison — compare any two time periods side-by-side (this month vs. last month, this year vs. last year)
  • Mileage Log — all mileage entries with IRS deduction totals

Filtering Reports

Most reports respect the Tax Year selector. For the full transaction-based reports, use the date range filters in the Transactions view before exporting.

13. Settings

Access via the gear icon (⚙) at the bottom of the sidebar. The Settings modal has six tabs:

  • Assets — add capital equipment (TrackMan unit, buildout improvements). The app calculates straight-line depreciation and Section 179 for your CPA
  • Loans — your SBA and TrackMan loans come pre-loaded. Update amounts as needed. The Loan Amortization report uses this data
  • Recurring — manage recurring transaction templates. Toggle active/inactive per template
  • Budgets — set monthly spending targets per category
  • Categories — add custom categories if none of the 23 defaults fit. Also set QuickBooks account names for IIF export
  • Documents — business document vault. Store your LLC Certificate, EIN letter, SBA agreement, insurance certificates, and lease here. Accessible from any device

16. Tips & Best Practices

  • Use the Quick-Log widget on the Dashboard for fast one-liner expenses — it takes about 5 seconds
  • Photograph receipts immediately after a purchase — the app accepts iPhone photos directly from your camera roll
  • Set up recurring transactions for rent and the SBA payment — you'll never forget to log them
  • Import your bank CSV once a month to catch anything you missed logging manually
  • If unsure which category to use, type the vendor name and the app will suggest one
  • The business bank account is your source of truth — personal card charges should be reimbursed and logged so both sets of books reconcile
  • Log even small amounts — your CPA will reconcile against bank statements and unexplained gaps slow down the process
  • Loan payments: always split interest vs. principal in More Options. Only interest is tax-deductible; your CPA needs to see this breakdown
  • For the SBA loan, set up a recurring template and activate it when the first payment is due in July 2026
  • The TrackMan financing starts February 2027 at $2,937/month — set that recurring template active when the time comes
  • Keep your document vault current — LLC Certificate, EIN letter, SBA agreement, lease, and insurance certificates should all be in there
  • Every quarter, check the Tax Alerts section on the Dashboard for estimated tax due dates
