Privacy Policy

Strikepoint Simulators, LLC · Last Updated: April 28, 2026

1. Introduction

Strikepoint Simulators, LLC (“Strikepoint,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) operates an autonomous indoor golf simulator facility and website at https://strikepointsims.com (the “Services”). The facility operates without on-site staff. Access is granted via a cloud-based keyless entry system, and customer support is provided through an AI-assisted platform. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and process personal data in compliance with the Connecticut Data Privacy Act (CTDPA) and other applicable law.

2. Scope

This policy applies to personal data collected through our website, waitlist and contact forms, membership enrollment, reservations, facility access, video surveillance, AI-assisted customer support, and all other interactions with our Services.

3. Personal Data We Collect

3a. Data You Provide Directly

  • Name, email address, phone number, and town or general location
  • Membership tier, reservation history, and account credentials
  • Waiver acknowledgment, including assumption of risk, liability release, consent to video recording, and photo/video release for marketing purposes
  • Communications submitted through our AI-assisted support platform, waitlist form, or email

3b. Facility Access Data

Access to the facility is managed through OpenPath, a cloud-based keyless entry system. OpenPath logs entry and exit events tied to individual access credentials. We retain access logs for 12 months, after which records are deleted. OpenPath may retain data on its own servers in accordance with its own privacy policy, which is available at https://www.openpath.com/legal/privacy-policy.

3c. Video Surveillance

The facility is monitored by security cameras covering the entrance, movement areas, and simulator bays. Continuous recording serves safety, loss prevention, and incident documentation purposes. Non-incident footage is retained for 30 days and then overwritten. Footage associated with a documented incident is archived indefinitely for insurance, legal, and law enforcement purposes. Video surveillance notices are posted at the facility entrance and within the bay area. Footage is not shared with third parties except as required by law or in connection with an incident under investigation.

3d. Trackman Session and Performance Data

Our simulator bays operate on the Trackman iO platform. When customers use the simulators, Trackman may collect session data, shot data, performance analytics, and account information through its platform. Trackman acts as an independent data controller for data it collects directly. As the equipment operator, we are required under Trackman’s terms and conditions to inform customers that their data is subject to Trackman’s Privacy Policy, available at https://www.trackman.com/legal/privacy-policy. Trackman retains customer data for the duration of the account relationship and for 24 months following account termination. Upon expiration of that retention period, Trackman may irreversibly anonymize performance and device data for internal research and development purposes.

With respect to data we hold independently (such as session records associated with a customer’s account in our own systems): we retain that data for the duration of the customer relationship. If a customer cancels their membership, their session records remain on file unless a deletion request is submitted. Upon a verified deletion request, we will remove personal data from our systems within 30 days.

3e. AI-Assisted Customer Support

Customer inquiries submitted through our text or web chat support interface are processed using Claude, an AI platform operated by Anthropic, PBC. Conversations may include names, reservation details, account questions, and other information you choose to share. We retain conversation logs for 90 days for quality assurance and knowledge base maintenance. The owner reviews logs on a periodic basis. Anthropic’s handling of data submitted to its platform is governed by Anthropic’s privacy policy, available at https://www.anthropic.com/legal/privacy.

3f. Payment Data

Payment processing is handled by Stripe, Inc. We do not store full credit card numbers, CVV codes, or bank account information. Stripe’s privacy policy is available at https://stripe.com/privacy.

3g. Automatically Collected Data

  • IP address, browser type, device type, and operating system
  • Website usage data, including pages visited, time on page, and referral source
  • Cookie and tracking technology data (see Section 10)

4. How We Use Personal Data

  • Granting and managing facility access
  • Processing reservations and membership accounts
  • Processing payments through Stripe
  • Responding to customer inquiries and support requests via AI-assisted chat
  • Sending transactional communications including booking confirmations, access credentials, and account notices
  • Sending marketing communications, including email and SMS, where consent has been provided
  • Running targeted advertising through Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and Google
  • Monitoring facility safety and security through video surveillance
  • Maintaining and improving our Services
  • Complying with applicable law and defending legal claims

We do not use personal data to train AI systems, and we do not sell personal data for monetary consideration. Certain disclosures to advertising platforms (Meta, Google) may qualify as a “sale” or “sharing” under Connecticut law. See Section 6 for opt-out rights.

5. Sharing of Personal Data

We may share personal data with the following categories of recipients:

  • Service providers acting on our behalf, including Stripe (payments), OpenPath (access control), Trackman (simulator platform), Anthropic (AI support), and similar vendors
  • Advertising platforms, including Meta and Google, for the purpose of delivering targeted advertising and building lookalike audiences
  • Law enforcement or government authorities when required by applicable law, court order, or legal process
  • Successor entities in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all business assets

We do not share personal data with third parties for their own independent marketing purposes. Mobile opt-in data and SMS consent are never shared with any third party for any purpose.

6. Connecticut Privacy Rights (CTDPA)

Connecticut residents have the following rights under the Connecticut Data Privacy Act:

  • Right to confirm whether we process your personal data and to access that data
  • Right to correct inaccuracies in your personal data
  • Right to delete personal data you have provided or that we have collected about you
  • Right to obtain a portable copy of your personal data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to opt out of targeted advertising, the sale of personal data, and profiling in furtherance of decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects

To exercise any of these rights, submit a request to with the subject line “Privacy Rights Request.” We will respond within 45 days. If additional time is required, we will notify you. We will not discriminate against you for exercising these rights.

7. Appeals

If we decline to act on a privacy rights request, you may appeal that decision by emailing with the subject line “Privacy Appeal.” We will respond within 60 days. If your appeal is denied, you may submit a complaint to the Connecticut Attorney General at https://portal.ct.gov/AG.

8. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach involving personal data, we will notify affected Connecticut residents and the Connecticut Attorney General in accordance with the Connecticut Data Breach Notification Law (Conn. Gen. Stat. § 36a-701b). Notification will be made in the most expedient time possible, and no later than 60 days following discovery of the breach, unless law enforcement determines that notification would impede a criminal investigation.

9. Minors

Our Services are not directed at individuals under the age of 18 as independent account holders. Minors may use the facility when accompanied by a parent or legal guardian who has completed the required waiver on the minor’s behalf and maintains supervision throughout the session. We do not knowingly collect personal data directly from minors. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected personal data from a minor without appropriate parental authorization, we will delete that data promptly.

10. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies, including Google Analytics and Meta Pixel. These tools collect data about website activity to support advertising, analytics, and service improvement. You may manage cookie preferences through your browser settings or using the on this site. Opting out of cookies may affect the functionality of certain website features.

Meta Pixel is used to measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns and to build custom and lookalike audiences on the Meta platform. If you prefer to opt out of Meta’s use of your data for advertising, visit https://www.facebook.com/settings/?tab=ads or use the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out tool at https://optout.aboutads.info.

11. Opt-Out of Targeted Advertising

To opt out of targeted advertising or the sharing of your data with advertising platforms, you may:

  • Email with the subject line “Opt-Out Request”
  • Use the ad preference settings on the Meta platform (https://www.facebook.com/settings/?tab=ads) or Google (https://adssettings.google.com)
  • Use the Digital Advertising Alliance opt-out tool at https://optout.aboutads.info

12. Sensitive Personal Data

We do not intentionally collect sensitive personal data as defined under the CTDPA, including racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, mental or physical health diagnoses, sexual orientation, immigration status, genetic or biometric data, or precise geolocation data. If we determine that sensitive data has been inadvertently collected, we will obtain opt-in consent before processing it, or delete it.

13. Data Retention

We retain personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, or as required by applicable law. The following general retention periods apply:

  • Active customer account data: retained for the duration of the membership or account relationship
  • Session records (independently held): retained unless a deletion request is received; deleted within 30 days of a verified request
  • AI support conversation logs: 90 days
  • Facility access logs (OpenPath): 12 months
  • Non-incident surveillance footage: 30 days (overwritten on a rolling basis)
  • Incident-related surveillance footage: retained indefinitely
  • Waiver records: retained for a minimum of 7 years to support potential legal claims
  • Transaction and payment records: retained as required by applicable tax and accounting obligations

Trackman’s independent retention of customer data is governed by Trackman’s Privacy Policy and is not within our control.

14. Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal data against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. Payment data is handled exclusively by Stripe and is not stored on our systems. No data transmission or storage system is guaranteed to be completely secure. In the event of a breach, we will follow the notification procedures described in Section 8.

15. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technology, legal requirements, or other factors. Material changes will be communicated by posting the revised policy on our website with an updated effective date. Continued use of the Services following the posting of changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
