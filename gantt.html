Key Observations — Through Year End

Cash-flow positive by November. October is the only operating month with negative cash flow ($2,700 deficit). By November, revenue ($15K) exceeds operating expenses ($13.3K) and the business is self-sustaining from an operating standpoint.

Cumulative position reaches ~$0 by December. The owner invests ~$27K peak in June, gets made whole when SBA funds in July, then the operating losses in Oct eat into reserves slightly before Nov-Dec surplus brings cumulative back to roughly break-even.

Trackman repayment is the elephant. The $70,485 due ~Feb 2027 is covered if SBA proceeds are reserved. The "SBA unspent reserve" memo line tracks this — by Dec there's ~$47K earmarked, meaning you need another ~$23K from Jan-Feb operating cash flow or personal funds to fully repay. Jan-Feb are peak season months, so this should be achievable if member growth stays on track.

Debt service is lower under new structure. Old model: $2,673/mo. New: $1,941/mo (SBA only). That's $732/mo savings = $8,784/yr, which flows straight to the bottom line. But the Trackman lump sum repayment replaces the steady monthly payments — it's a different cash flow shape, not necessarily better total cost.

Model updates still needed: (1) Architect $6K→$10K, (2) Rent abatement 2→3 months, (3) TI $61K→$40K, (4) SBA $100K→$150K, (5) Trackman 0% structure, (6) Debt service recalc, (7) Monthly projection buildout month opex.
